Bizzard

What is a Bizzard?

Severe winter storms that are accompanied by heavy snow and strong winds are so-called blizzards or snowstorms. When very strong winds stir up the snow that has already fallen and accumulated then we speak of a ground blizzard. Snowstorms can cause great damage and destruction, especially in areas and regions where they are rare. This can negatively affect the meateconomy.

What causes Bizzards and where do they happen?

Snow forms when two air masses collide one with warm air and one with cold air they collide and make a front if there is more cold air it creates a snow if there is more warm air it will just rain. When this snow is combined with the wind, and decreased visibility, it forms a blizzard.

What is the biggest Bizzard in the world history?



One of the largest and first recorded and described blizzards (snowstorms) was in 1888 affected the United States and led to the destruction of huge amounts of crops. Other huge blizzards were also recorded in 1972. In Iran, in 1993, the so-called "super storm" which started from the Gulf of Mexico and traveled northwards, causing damage to 26 states and also damage in Canada and caused the death of over 300 people. Another such severe blizzard was recorded in Asia in Afghanistan in 2008 which caused very extensive damage.
